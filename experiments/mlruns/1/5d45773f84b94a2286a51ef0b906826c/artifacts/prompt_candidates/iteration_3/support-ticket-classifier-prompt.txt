Instructions for Classifying Customer Support Messages:

1. **Objective**:
   Classify a customer support message into **exactly one** of the four categories: Incident, Request, Problem, or Change.

2. **Categories**:
   - **Incident**:
     - Definition: An unexpected issue that requires immediate attention.
     - Characteristics: Typically involves urgent matters, unexpected disruptions, and may require swift resolution.
     - Identifying Factors: Look for urgency keywords such as "immediate," "urgent," "outage," or similar terms indicating a crisis situation needing immediate action.

   - **Request**:
     - Definition: A routine inquiry or service request.
     - Characteristics: Involves questions, general information inquiries, assistance, or seeking clarification on existing services without urgency.
     - Identifying Factors: Focus on inquiries or asking for information regarding services, features, or routine processes without urgency or immediate action needed.

   - **Problem**:
     - Definition: An underlying or systemic issue causing multiple incidents.
     - Characteristics: Refers to issues affecting multiple users or systems, indicating potential underlying root causes requiring investigation.
     - Identifying Factors: Look for mentions of systemic failures or multiple user impacts, such as "billing problems with several products," "payment gateway malfunction," or references to root cause analysis.

   - **Change**:
     - Definition: A planned change, update, or configuration request.
     - Characteristics: Involves requests for updates to existing services or configurations, planned changes in settings, billing information, payment details, or any modifications to active accounts or systems.
     - Identifying Factors: Keywords such as "update," "modify," "change billing information," "update settings," or similar terms indicate a proactive change or configuration request.

3. **Classification Process**:
   - Carefully read the provided customer support message.
   - Analyze the content to determine the primary purpose and context of the message.
   - Identify keywords or phrases that match the characteristics or definitions of each category. 
   - Consider the nuances and choose the category that best fits the context of the message, ensuring it aligns with the predefined characteristics.

4. **Output**:
   - Return **only** the category name as either "Incident," "Request," "Problem," or "Change."
   - Do not provide explanation or additional text beyond the single category name.

5. **Examples**:
   - A message seeking to update billing information: "Change"
   - An inquiry about pricing plans and subscription details: "Request"
   - A communication reporting a systemic failure impacting multiple accounts: "Problem"
   - A report of a website outage requiring immediate attention: "Incident"

6. **Review**:
   - Evaluate the feedback from previous classifications to ensure continual improvement and accuracy.
   - Adjust your interpretation as necessary based on contextual clues and any additional guidance provided in feedback for future tasks.
