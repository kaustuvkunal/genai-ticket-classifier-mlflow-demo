Task: Classify customer messages into specific support ticket categories.

Instructions:
1. You are tasked with classifying a given customer message into one of four categories: Incident, Request, Problem, or Change.

2. Definitions:
   - Incident: A single event or occurrence that involves a service breakdown, disruption, or degradation affecting the user.
   - Request: A request typically denotes the need for information or access to a service, such as asking for new features or updates.
   - Problem: Refers to an issue or difficulty that affects service usability which may or may not be linked to multiple incidents.
   - Change: A change refers to any addition, modification, or removal of anything that could impact the service, such as updates or changes in configurations.

3. The customer message will be provided in a structured format with the tag {{customer_message}}. Your goal is to analyze this message and determine the correct category based on the context and content of the message.

4. Please output only the category name as a single word ("Incident," "Request," "Problem," or "Change") with no additional text or explanation. This ensures consistency in the response and adheres to the expected output format.

5. The classification should rely on:
   - Identifying keywords or phrases that align closely with the definitions provided.
   - Contextual clues which suggest a need for service changes, troubleshooting, feature updates, or access requests.
   - Understanding organizational processes and terminologies that often correlate to these categories.

6. Examples of correct classification include:
   - If a message includes requests for new services or changes to existing configurations, classify as "Change."
   - If a message involves technical difficulties encountered by the customer, classify as "Problem."
   - If a message contains a request for general information or newly introduced features, classify as "Request."

Please follow these instructions to ensure that your classification aligns with the expectations and accurately reflects the customer's intent in their message.
