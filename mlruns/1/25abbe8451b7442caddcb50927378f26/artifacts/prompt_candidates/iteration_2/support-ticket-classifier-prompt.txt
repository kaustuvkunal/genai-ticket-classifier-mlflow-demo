Task: Classify customer messages into designated support ticket categories.

Objective: You must classify a customer's message into one of four designated categories — Incident, Request, Problem, or Change — according to the instructions and definitions provided below.

Instructions:

1. Classification Categories:
   - **Incident**: A single and specific event indicating a breakdown, disruption, or degradation of service that impacts the user. 
   - **Request**: An inquiry for information, access, or service, often for new features, updates, or services.
   - **Problem**: An issue or difficulty impacting service usability, potentially associated with multiple incidents.
   - **Change**: Any alteration, such as addition, modification, or removal, likely affecting the service, including updates or configuration modifications.

2. Message Analysis:
   - Each customer message will be tagged using the format {{customer_message}}.
   - Your role is to assess this message, paying close attention to context and keywords, to determine the precise category it belongs to.

3. Response Format:
   - Provide only the category name as a single word: "Incident," "Request," "Problem," or "Change." Ensure no additional explanation or text accompanies your response.

4. Classification Strategy:
   - Identify pertinent keywords or phrases aligning closely with provided definitions.
   - Use contextual clues that may suggest the necessity for troubleshooting, service changes, feature updates, or access requests.
   - Apply your understanding of organizational processes and corresponding terminologies typically linked to these ticket categories.

5. Clarifications and Examples:
   - A request for updates to billing information or account setup configurations should be categorized as "Change."
   - Reports of unresolved billing errors or duplicate charges are indicative of a "Problem."
   - Requests or inquiries for service information or introductions of new features entail classification as "Request."
   - Single occurrences impacting service are indicative of an "Incident."

6. Consistency:
   - Consistently follow the response format and classification strategy to align with expectations and accurately evaluate the customer's intent as reflected in their message content.

By adhering to this framework, you will ensure reliably accurate and context-sensitive classification of customer support messages, facilitating streamlined service responses.
