Task: Classify customer messages into specific support ticket categories.

Instructions:

1. **Objective**: You are tasked with classifying a given customer message into one of four categories: Incident, Request, Problem, or Change.

2. **Definitions**:
   - **Incident**: A single service-related event or occurrence involving a breakdown, disruption, or degradation affecting the user.
   - **Request**: A message that indicates a need for information or access to a service, such as inquiries about new features, general information, or updates.
   - **Problem**: An issue or difficulty affecting service usability, which may or may not be linked to multiple incidents.
   - **Change**: Any addition, modification, or removal impacting the service, like configuration updates, feature changes, billing updates, or integrations.

3. **Classification Guidelines**:
   - Identify keywords or phrases closely aligning with the provided definitions.
   - Use context to suggest a need for service changes, troubleshooting, feature updates, access requests, or other specific actions.
   - Understand organizational processes and terminology that often relate to these categories.

4. **Output Requirements**: 
   - Output should be only the category name as a single word: "Incident," "Request," "Problem," or "Change."
   - No additional text or explanations should accompany the category to ensure consistency.

5. **Examples**:
   - If a message includes requests for new services, changes to existing configurations, or billing updates, classify as "Change."
   - If a message involves technical difficulties or issues encountered by the customer, classify as "Problem."
   - If a message requests general information or details about newly introduced features or payment options, classify as "Request."

6. **General Strategy**:
   - Focus on determining the primary intent of the customer’s message through its content and context.
   - Use contextual clues and terminological familiarity to differentiate between categories accurately.
   - Ensure every classification accurately reflects the customer’s intent as communicated in the message.

7. **Examples of Correct Classification**:
   - "Kindly update the billing integration for seamless payments." should be classified as "Change."
   - "I would like to request information about the payment methods accepted for our subscription plans." should be classified as "Request."

Please follow these instructions to ensure that your classification aligns with the expectations and accurately reflects the customer's intent in their message.
